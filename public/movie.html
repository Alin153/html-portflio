Series Rankings

By Baluta Paul Alin

Breaking Bad

Throughout the series, Walter, who adopts the alias "Heisenberg," transforms from a simple, moral man into a ruthless drug lord. The show delves into complex themes such as moral corruption, the devastating effects of organized crime, and the impact of drugs on the community. The performances of Bryan Cranston (Walter White) and Aaron Paul (Jesse Pinkman) were highly acclaimed, contributing to the series' success and critical recognition. "Breaking Bad" is considered one of the greatest television series of all time.

Better call Saul

Set in Albuquerque, New Mexico, "Better Call Saul" explores Jimmy's complex relationships, especially with his brother Chuck McGill, a respected lawyer who suffers from a debilitating condition, and Kim Wexler, a fellow lawyer and Jimmy's love interest. The series also delves into the backstories of other key characters from "Breaking Bad," such as Mike Ehrmantraut and Gustavo Fring, enriching the narrative with deeper context and connections.

Game of Thrones

"Game of Thrones" is a fantasy TV series that aired on HBO from 2011 to 2019, based on George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" novels.

Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, it follows noble families such as the Starks, Lannisters, and Targaryens as they vie for control of the Iron Throne. Key characters include Ned Stark, Daenerys Targaryen, Jon Snow, Cersei Lannister, and Tyrion Lannister. The show is known for its complex political intrigue, epic battles, and elements of magic and mysticism.
